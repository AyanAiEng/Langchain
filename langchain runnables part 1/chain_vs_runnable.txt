FILE: Difference Between Chain and Runnable in LangChain

CHAIN (Old Approach) - Predefined workflow structure - Less flexible -
Hard to customize steps - Example: LLMChain, SimpleSequentialChain

RUNNABLE (Modern Approach) - Everything is a composable unit - Highly
flexible and modular - Supports: - parallel execution - branching
logic - streaming - Example: prompt | llm | parser

KEY DIFFERENCE: - Chain = fixed structure - Runnable = building blocks
you can combine freely

CONCLUSION: Runnables replaced chains because they are more powerful,
flexible, and scalable.
